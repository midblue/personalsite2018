ADRIANA DAVID


Adriana David graduated from the Universidad Iberoamericana with a degree in Architecture and Urbanism. She founded with Guillermo Gonzalez the architecture office Tiliche. In 2009 the Instituto Europeo Di Design, in Madrid, awarded her a full scholarship to study a Master in Scenographic Design. In 2011, she founded the design studio DOMA, a workshop for the experimentation of sustainable spaces and techniques. At DOMA, Adriana designed and built the experimental seed bank for Canasta de Semillas A.C. and collaborated with the design office TOA (Taller de Operaciones Ambientales), among others.

During the year 2012, she was an advisor on museographic projects at the Papalote Children’s Museum in Mexico City. In 2013 she founded Hojas De Te s.a. de c.v., an experimentation center whose objective is to create integral projects of space exploration integrating different artistic disciplines: dance, theater, architecture. Adriana was a recipient of the FONCA Young Creators Scholarship 2013 - 2014 and is currently pursuing her master’s degree in design studies Art Design and the Public Domain at Harvard University.

doma.mx

ANDRES SOUTO


mUcHo estudio/taller is a multidisciplinary studio and workshop with a focus on architecture, art, teaching and research. They do architecture, interior design, landscaping, furniture, illustration, painting, sculpture, engraving among many other things all governed by architectural research. They investigate architecture made outside the parameters of official culture, exploring the methods and processes in which popular culture interprets and appropriates official architecture and speculate on how these might evolve.

mUcHo studio/workshop was founded by Architect Andres Souto in 2017 after studying his master’s degree at the Royal College of Art in London. It was there that the focus of his practice was shaped and defined through his research project entitled “The Aesthetics of Hope”. Andres Souto is a lecturer in the Department of Architecture at the Universidad Iberoamericana where he has been teaching the architecture studio “Architecture of Popular Culture” - @arqdelaculpop - since 2017.

muchomuchomucho.mx

ERIKA LOANA


Erika Loana is a multidisciplinary architect. Her explorations are born from the idea of ​​the void and abandonment resulting from sudden changes experienced by large cities. She is part of the collective Somosmexas, a group of young architects working in and for the city. It is run through ATEA, a multidisciplinary experimental space where Erika coordinates exhibitions and installations, in addition to carrying out her own urban investigation projects.

She began her career working in the offices of Taller de Arquitectura–Mauricio Rocha. Erika has specialized in museographic productions for which she has collaborated with artists such as Minerva Cuevas, Demian Flores, and Perla Krauze while working at the Museum of the City of Mexico. She was fellow of the FONCA “Young Artists” Scholarship 2011-2012 with the project “Terrain Vague”. Loana has been coordinator of architectural and museum projects in the Ministry of Culture of the State of Morelos in collaboration with architects Mauricio Rocha, Issac Broid, Javier Sanchez JSA. and PRODUCTORA; and has been exhibition designer at the Jumex Museum of Contemporary Art.

somosmexa.tumblr.mx

LANZA ATELIER (Alessandro Arienzo & Isabel Abascal)


The architecture studio LANZA Atelier was founded in 2015 by Isabel Abascal and Alessandro Arienzo. LANZA has been nominated for the Debut Award of the Lisbon Triennale 2019, the 2016 Ibero-American Architecture Biennial Award and the Mies Crown Hall Award for Emerging Architects, IIT Chicago 2016. The studio is one of the winners of the Architectural League Prize of 2017. LANZA’s first solo show took place at SFMOMA in 2018.

The architecture of LANZA Atelier is conceived as the construction of a contemporary space whose energy can last forever. Still managing to function as a flexible artifact that accommodates changes of use and eventually changes of place, we like to think that we are designing a ruin.

lanzaatelier.com

ZAICKZ MOZ


Originally from the State of Mexico, Zaickz Moz entered the Faculty of Architecture of the UNAM (2012). After doing an internship in various offices in Mexico City, he came to photography. His interest in learning to portray architectural space led him to Onnis Luque (2015), a fundamental step in his formal approach to the camera and the post-production process. He left the drawing and the plans to move on to build the city and the spaces through other means.

As an architectural photographer his career released him towards interests that at times he thought were everyday but that hid the depth of a documentary vocation, which reconnected him with the subject that has most fascinated him until now: the city.

zaickzstudio.com

JONATHAN HAGARD


Jonathan Hagard is a half French Indonesian director, animator and illustrator based in Kyoto, Japan. Between documentary and animated fiction, based on his experiences and researches in sprawling cities such as Tokyo and Jakarta, he tells stories through traditional animation and new media. His recent work, Replacement, was selected by Venice Film Festival 2020.

jonathanhagard.com

KEITH SPENCER


Keith Spencer (b.1982, New Mexico) is a visual artist, writer, and educator. Keith obtained a Post-Baccalaureate Certificate in Painting and Drawing from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago (Chicago, IL) in 2016, and completed his MFA at Emily Carr University of Art + Design (Vancouver, BC) in 2019. In 2009, Keith was named a goodwill ambassador for Fukushima prefecture, Japan and in 2016 received The Mary Koga Memorial Grant from the Japan America Society of Chicago. Keith was a 2019 Emily Carr University of Art + Design Teaching Fellow whose work has been shown in the US, Japan, and Canada. He is a current artist-in-residence at KAGANHOTEL in Kyoto, Japan.

MEI NISHIYAMA


Born in 1989 in Gunma, Japan, Mei is a director of Mikey Ltd. and a representative of HELLO GARDEN and Nishichiba Studio. After studying architecture at Chiba University, her experience included various projects as a planner at Kitayama & Company, and she eventually launched HELLO GARDEN and Nishichiba Studio in the city of Nishichiba. She then joined Mikey Ltd. in 2014 to take full responsibility for the operation and management of these spaces. She also works as an adviser to aid in various city-making projects across Japan.

hellogarden.jp

NO ARCHITECTS (Hiroshi Nishiyama & Keiko Okudaira)


NO ARCHITECTS was founded in 2011 by Hiroshi Nishiyama and Keiko Okudaira. They first began working as a duo called nishiyamahiroshiokudairakeiko after finishing their respective graduate degrees at Kobe Design University. They launched NO ARCHITECTS as they moved their office to Konohana, Osaka. Keeping architectural practices at the core, their work involves different activities ranging across design, installation, workshops, layout design, and city-making. They collaborate with musicians, artists, photographers, playwriters, poets, and dancers through multi-disciplinary projects such as exhibition planning.

noarchitects.jp

REINA IMAGAWA


Reina Imagawa (b.1992) is a Japanese designer, architect, trilingual social engineer and arts leader based in Mexico City. She looks critically at models of social participation embedded in art, design and urban systems, exploring the role design could play in breaking these traditional paradigms while working with existing systems of power. Her recent work combines people, architecture and public programming to produce designs that realign hierarchy and agency.

Her public programs and installations have occupied institutions such as Museo Tamayo and the Museo Universitario de Ciencias y Arte in Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Mexico (UNAM). Her people-centered practice has been recognized by Core77 Design Awards, the Los Angeles County Arts Commission, Placemaking Latino America Mexico and WIRED Japan.

reinaimagawa.com

KAREN AGUILAR(MX)


Karen Aguilar Martinez was born on June 13, 1990 in Mexico City. She has a degree in Design and Visual Communication from the Faculty of Arts and Design (FAD-UNAM). She obtained recognition in the Second Edition of the Latin American Illustration Award; in the XXIII and XXVII Catalog of Illustrators of Children and Youth Publications and in the XXV and XXIX National Invite to Read Poster Contest. She was selected in the Create Art for Paper Towns contest (United States) and won Second Place in the Let’s Make Time Together contest (Japan). Freelance designer and communicator since 2015 focused on Illustration, Graphic Design and Animation.

MONICA GARCIA (MX)


Graduated in architecture from the Universidad Autonoma del Estado de Hidalgo,
founded in August 2019 VISTA IN PLANT: A creative project that merges illustration and architecture as an alternative graphic representation. She currently resides in Mexico, promoting illustration, collaborating with artists and architectural firms as well as giving workshops.

ALAN RODRIGUEZ (MX)

RIICHIRO SHINOZAKI (JP)


Born in 1989 in Japan. While studying mathematics at university, he started making drawings with a pen. He selects landscapes, townscapes, cityscapes, people, nature, etc, which are mainly seen in everyday life as elements from artifacts to natural objects, and combines them multilaterally to draw a single motif.

THE JAPAN FOUNDATION, MEXICO


The Japan Foundation (Kokusai Kouryu Kikin) is a special entity created in 1972 with the aim of promoting Japanese culture in the world, as well as carrying out cultural exchange activities at an international level. The office of The Japan Foundation, Mexico began its activities in 1987 with the purpose of carrying out cultural exchange activities, as well as carrying out the programs organized by the headquarters in Tokyo.

In addition to introducing and publicizing Japanese culture in Mexico, it promotes friendship and mutual understanding in the international arena, providing support for the development and dissemination of Japanese language teaching and studies about Japan in the area of social sciences and humanities in the institutions and research centers that develop these activities.

BRIDGE TO KYOTO


Founded by Mariko Sugita (editor and researcher) and Jasper Stephenson (designer), Bridge to Kyoto is a residency program for urbanists as well as an event, workshop, and gallery space in Kyoto, Japan. It is based in an old, postwar, Japanese-style home renovated in 2019, and is open to anyone who is interested in urbanism, architecture, and sustainable living. It is also a curatorial platform and publisher dedicated to the issues of urbanism and civic design along with its sister web platform Traveling Circus of Urbanism. Together, they organize workshops, lectures, talks, performances, and more to encourage multi-disciplinary collaboration between urbanists.

PROYECTOR


Proyector is a curatorial platform based in Mexico City, dedicated to promoting emerging voices in contemporary architectural research. Proyector is committed to fostering new strategies and critical, theoretical and historical tools on spatial issues. During the three seasons of the annual program, researchers (and research groups) are invited to work together with the curatorial team of Proyector in a collaborative manner, seeking the projection of their research in multiple formats: exhibitions, publications, workshops, performances, talks, seminars, among others.
